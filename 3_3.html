2/23 Past necessities: using “have to” in the past

Have to sirve para hablar acerca de una obligación que aún tienes opción a realizarla, es decir, no es obligatoria de cumplir.

Para utilizar have to en una oración para referirse a una obligación es necesario tener en cuenta lo siguiente:

  • Es necesario preguntar en pasado.
    Estructura: Did you have to + verb ?
  • Para responder en negativo se utiliza el verbo auxiliar did not.
  • Para responder en positivo se utiliza el verbo had.

Ejemplos usando have to

(?) Did you have to do your homework?
(-) No, I didn’t

(?) Did you have to do the laundry?
(+) Yes, I had to.

(?) Did you have to finish your project?
(+) Yes, I had to.

(?) Did you have to wash the dishes?
(+/-) Yes, but I didn’t do them and now my mom is upset.

5/23 Expressing quantities with "hardly" and "not much"

Hardly y barely son adverbios para expresar cantidad de algo, específicamente una cantidad muy poca, casi nada. También puedes utilizar not much y not many para sustantivos incontables y contables, respectivamente.

Ejemplos para expresar cantidades casi nulas

  • I hardly watch TV.
  • He barely does the laundry.
  • There is not much time left.
  • There are not many sports left.

Hardly, barely, not much/many

Hardly anyone came to the meeting! (almost no one came)

We went to our friend’s house and there was hardly anything to eat.

He now lives in a different country so we hardly ever see him, but we try to keep in touch.

7/23 Other and another

Did you know that English has two different words to express “otherness”? Let’s have a look at the differences between other and another:

Another

Another means ‘one more’ or ‘an additional or extra’, or ‘an alternative or different’. It is always used with a singular noun.

For example:

  1. I would like another juice, it was really good!
    Here, another means ‘one more’.

  2. I want to see another option before buying this bike.
    Here, another means ‘an alternative or different’.

Other

Other can be used with either a plural or a singular noun. The other with a singular noun means ‘the second of two things or people’, or ‘the opposite of a set of two’. Similarly, other followed by a plural noun means ‘things that are different from the ones already mentioned’.

For example:

  1. This is a nice cellphone, but the other cellphone is cheaper!
    Here, the other cellphone means ‘the opposite of a set of two’.

  2. Well, three of my friends arrived, but I don’t know where my other friends are. Maybe they got stuck in traffic.
    Here, my other friends means ‘my friends that are different from the ones that arrived’.

  2. The _______ movie is so much better than this one.
  3. Do you know _______ lawyer?
  4. Some of the _______ players will arrive later tonight.

10/23 Until and till

Let’s sort this out once and for all: is there a difference between until and till? Well, let’s find out!

Until is a preposition that expresses that something will happen up to a given time. That is to say, some action has started and will end at the point in time mentioned.

For example, we may say:

  1. Can you wait until I finish eating my lunch?
  2. The show will be live until midnight.

So, what about till?
Till can be used in all the same ways as until, but it is perceived as less formal. This formality is really the only difference between the two words.

Some sentences in which we might use till are:

  1. I’m going out till the sun comes out.
  2. I’ll be out of town till tomorrow.


11/23 Using the present simple to refer to the future

Como ya sabrás, present simple es una forma verbal para referirse a acciones que suceden en el presente en un instante determinado. Sin embargo, también lo podemos usar para referirnos al futuro, mediante un horario establecido.

Existen situaciones que su escritura está en presente, pero su significado está en un futuro. Por ejemplo, “El tren llega mañana a las 5:00 pm”, “Mi equipo favorito juega la próxima semana”.

A partir de esto, ¡practiquemos!

12/23 Prohibition using the present and near future

Must / musn’t sirve para hablar acerca de una regla u orden, algo que es obligatorio de realizar. Puede usarse particularmente para prohibir algo en un lugar.

Ejemplos usando must/musn’t

What mustn’t we do in the restaurant?

  • We mustn’t leave without paying.
  • We must be respectful to the server.
  • We mustn’t throw the food in the air.

What must we do at school or university?

  • We must respect the teacher.
  • We must do our homework.
  • We must be on time.

What must we do at the airport?

  • We must bring our passport or identity card?
  • We must be on time.
  • We must be patient, because sometimes there are a lot of delays.

What must we do at the park?

  • We mustn’t throw garbage on the ground.
  • We must have fun.
  • We mustn’t damage the plants.

13/23 Speculations

May, might y could son verbos modales que sirven para especular sobre acciones del futuro; y representan una probabilidad alta, media y baja, respectivamente. Tener en cuenta que no se refieren a un evento que seguramente pasará.

  • It may rain tomorrow (seguro que lloverá).
  • It might rain tomorrow (tal vez llueva).
  • It could rain tomorrow (podría llover).

Ejemplos usando may, might y could

  • I may go to the gym tomorrow morning.
  • She might call him back.
  • They could leave one hour earlier to avoid the traffic jams.

15/23 First conditional

Un condicional consiste en que si sucede una primera acción, en consecuencia pasará una segunda acción. Existen cuatro formas de condicionales.

First conditional sirve para referirse a posibles situaciones en el futuro a partir de una acción del presente. Se utiliza la palabra clave if, seguido de las formas verbales present simple y future simple.

Las estructuras para formar un first conditional son las siguientes:

  • Future simple + if + present simple.
  • If + present simple, + future simple (ten en cuenta la coma entre las formas verbales).

Ejemplos usando first conditional

  • If it rains, I will use my umbrella.
  • If I am late to the meeting, I will text my coworker.
  • I will order out for delivery if I don’t have time to cook today.
  • She will stay late at the office working if she doesn’t finish the report.

Pratiquemos el uso del first conditional

Completa las siguientes oraciones con tus propias respuestas.

  • If I don’t finish my project …
  • If you don’t arrive on time …
  • If we don’t find the car keys …

Posibles respuestas

  • If I don’t finish my project, I won’t go to the cinema.
  • If you don’t arrive on time, I will leave the restaurant.
  • If we don’t find the car keys, we won’t be able to go on our trip.

17/23 Phrasal verbs

Los phrasal verbs indican una acción mediante la combinación entre un verbo y una preposición o un adverbio (up, down, off, on, away, for, etc.). Por ejemplos: check out, get in, get off, drop off, etc.

Ten en cuenta las siguientes recomendaciones:

  • Sé paciente, no te aprendas varios phrasal verbs a la vez.
  • Practica y asocia con ejemplos de la vida real.
  • Practica 1-2 veces por semana usándolos intencionalmente.

Los Phrasal verbs se clasifican en separables, es decir, que puedes utilizar un sustantivo entre el verbo y la preposición o adverbio; e inseparables, se los utiliza siempre de la misma forma.

Ejemplos usando phrasal verbs

Revisa los enlaces de los phrasal verbs para conocer su significado.

  • Put on the jacket.
  • She turned on the light.
  • Take off his hat.
  • We turned down the music.

Ejemplos usando phrasal verbs separables

✅ Drop off my sister at school.
✅ Drop my sister off at school.

✅ Clean up the kitchen.
✅ Clean the kitchen up.

Ejemplos usando phrasal verbs inseparables

✅ Check out of the hotel.
❌ Check the hotel out of.

✅ Get in the car.
❌ Get the car in.

✅ Get off the bed.
❌ Get the bed off.

18/23 Passive transitive verbs

El verbo es la acción que un sustantivo realiza (sujeto) o recibe (objeto) en la oración y existen diferentes tipos de verbos en inglés: de acción, modales, auxiliares, entre otros.

Los verbos transitivos pasivos son aquellos que el sujeto recibe la acción. Su estructura es una combinación entre el verbo to be y la acción conjugada en past participle.

Estructura: Sujeto + verbo to be + verbo en past participle + complemento.

Ejemplos usando verbos transitivos pasivos

  • The door was closed by Kelly.
  • The picture was painted by Botero.
  • The meal was prepared by my partner.
  • The match was won by the Lions.
  • The book was written by Ernest Hermingway.

19/23 Indefinite compound pronouns

Los pronombres impersonales compuestos son aquellos que se refieren a personas, lugares o cosas de manera general.

A los pronombres impersonales compuestos, aunque se refieran a un grupo, se les considera como tercera persona en singular (como he, she, it).

Things Places People
every everything everywhere everyone / everybody
any anything anywhere anyone / anybody
some something somewhere someone / somebody
no nothing nowhere no one / nobody

Every-

Every- se refiere a todas las personas, cosas o lugares.

  • Everything is good.
  • I can go everywhere.
  • Everybody came today.
  • Everyone needs to go.

Any-

Any- se refiere a todas las personas, cosas o lugares en una oración positiva, y también se refiere a ninguna en una oración negativa.

  • I can’t hear anything.
  • You can go anywhere you want.
  • Anybody can do it.
  • Does anyone need to go?

Some-

Some- se refiere a una cierta cantidad de personas, cosas o lugares.

  • Something is wrong.
  • I should go somewhere.
  • Somebody help me!
  • She was with someone yesterday.

No-

No- se refiere a ninguna persona, cosa o lugar en una oración positiva.

  • There is nothing to eat.
  • I can go nowhere with this weather.
  • Nobody cares.
  • No one is safe.

21/23 Connecting ideas with "when" whit past continuous and past simple

When, together with past continuous and past simple, can be used to connect two different ideas or actions. The clause in past continuous indicates that there is a longer or ongoing action, which is interrupted by a short action in past simple.

Let’s have a look at some examples:

  1. My cell phone rang when I was working.
    👉I was working, and then my cell phone rang.

  2. I was talking when my friend interrupted me.
    👉I was talking, and then my friend interrupted me.

  3. When I was waiting for the bus today, I saw her.
    👉I was waiting for the bus, and then I saw her.

22/23 Present perfect simple and continuous

Present perfect simple y present perfect continuous son dos formas gramaticales que hacen referencia a una acción que inició en pasado. Ambas utilizan el verbo auxiliar have / has en tiempo presente.

De manera general, la forma gramatical present perfect simple indica que la acción ha terminado; y present perfect continuous indica que la acción continúa en el presente.

Present perfect simple

Los puntos más importantes para utilizar el present perfect simple son:

  • Acciones terminadas.
  • Acciones permanentes.
  • El resultado es más importante.

Estructura: Subject + have/has + verb in past participle + complement.

Ejemplos usando present perfect simple

  • I have eaten at that restaurante before.
  • I have finished my homework!
  • He has called her three times already!
  • (?) Have you been to New York city?
    -Yes! I have been to New York city.

Present perfect continuous

Los puntos más importantes para utilizar el present perfect continuous son:

  • Acciones inconclusas, siguen sucediendo en el tiempo presente.
  • Acciones temporales (recently, lately).
  • Oraciones enfocadas a la repetición o duración de algo.

Estructura: Subject + have/has + been + verb in present participle (-ing) + complement.

Ejemplos usando present perfect simple

  • I have been taking painting lessons recently!
  • She has been studying English every day this week.
  • I have been calling her all morning.
  • (?) What have you been doing at work this week?
    -I have been working on a new project. I haven’t finished yet!
