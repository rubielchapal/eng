2/26 Live events

Durante los eventos en vivo (live events), un comentarista o presentador describe las acciones al mismo tiempo que estas suceden. Por lo tanto, la estructura gramatical para describir los eventos en vivo es presente simple.

Recuerda la conjugación del verbo para tercera persona singular.

Algunos ejemplos de eventos en vivos son: deportes, conciertos, inauguraciones, programas de televisión.

Ejemplos de eventos en vivo

Mira los siguientes eventos y responde: ¿qué está sucediendo?

Ejemplo 1

  • He passes the ball.
  • Zidane nutmegs.
  • He sees the ball.
  • He shoots and scores.

Ejemplo 2

  • She runs out of the house.
  • She carries a gun.
  • She jumps into a car.
  • She flees from the scene.

3/26 Sequence connectors

Los conectores son palabras que unen una idea con otra. A su vez, los conectores de secuencia indican el orden de ciertos eventos.

Algunos conectores de secuencia son:

  • First / Second / Third
  • To start with
  • First of all
  • For a start
  • Meanwhile
  • Later
  • Then
  • Next
  • Before / After
  • During
  • At this point
  • Eventually
  • In the end
  • Finally

Ejemplo usando conectores de secuencia

Lee el siguiente texto y observa los conectores de secuencia:

Rose is an upper-class woman who hates her relationship with her fiancé. One day, they go on a trip onboard a well-known ship. While sailing, she meets a poor man named Jack. They fall in love right after she threatens to jump off of the ship. They keep seeing each other, although Rose’s mother does not agree. In the end, the ship hits an iceberg and sinks. Jack dies. Eventually, Rose decides to tell this tragic love story to the world.

Ejercicio de writing

Escribe una pequeña descripción de alguna película o libro, utilizando varios conectores de secuencia.

5/26 Differences between past simple and present perfect

Past simple

● Finished actions
I bought this at the store 2 days ago.
She had lunch, and then she went home.
Mike was my neighbor for 20 years. He moved house last week.


Present perfect

● Indefinite time / Unfinished time.
● Experiences and achievements.
● Finished actions connected with the present.
● Recent events.
● Actions that started in the past and are still true.

Subject + have / has + verb (past participle)

Present perfect

● Finished actions connected with the present
He has lost his wallet, so I have to pay.
They’ll be late. They have missed the flight.
● Recent events
No, thanks. I have eaten already.
The president has given a great speech
recently.

● Actions that started in the past and are
still true
Mike has been my neighbor for 20
years. We are best friends.
They have lived there their whole life.

Past simple y present perfect son dos formas gramaticales que hacen referencia a una acción que inició en pasado.

Past simple

Estructura: subject + verb in past + complement.

Los puntos más importantes para utilizar el past simple son:

Tiempo definido y concluido:

  • I saw the Great Wall of China in 2002.
  • I went to her house yesterday.

Acciones repetidas en el pasado:

  • Every day he prepared their breakfast.

Acciones terminadas en el pasado:

  • I bought this at the store 2 days ago.
  • She had lunch, and then she went home.
  • Mike was my neighbor for 20 years. He moved house last week.

Present perfect

Estructura: subject + have/has + verb in past participle + complement.

Puedes relacionar el present perfect con la estructura gramatical del español llamada “pretérito perfecto”. Los puntos más importantes para utilizar el present perfect son:

Tiempo indefinido e inconcluso:

  • I have been to her house many times. (he estado)
  • She has worked there since 2010. (he trabajado)

Experiencias y logros:

  • I have seen the Great Wall of China. (he visto)
  • Bob has written many successful books. (ha escrito)

Acciones terminadas en el tiempo presente:

  • He has lost his wallet, so I have to pay. (ha perdido)
  • They’ll be late. They have missed the flight. (no han alcanzado)

Eventos recientes:

  • No, thanks. I have eaten already. (he comido)
  • The president has given a great speech recently. (ha entregado)

Acciones que comenzaron en el pasado y siguien siendo ciertas:

  • Mike has been my neighbor for 20 years. We are best friends. (ha sido)
  • They have lived there their whole life. (han vivido)

6/26 Past habits

Used to

● Something that is no longer true.
● State, situation, or repeated action.

Subject + used to + verb (infinitive)

I used to play soccer every weekend.
She used to have short hair when she was a
kid.
John used to smoke. He quit last month.
They used to take the bus to work.

Be careful!

Subj. + didn’t use to + verb

Did + subj. + use to + verb?

They used to take the bus to work.
They didn’t use to take the bus to work.
Did they use to take the bus to work?

Usamos las palabras ‘used to’ para hablar sobre hábitos del pasado: cosas que hicimos alguna vez pero que no practicamos en el presente.

La estructura para este tipo de oraciones es: subject + used to + base form of the verb

Por ejemplo

  • They used to take the bus to work, but now they work from home.
  • I used to play basketball when I was a kid, but I had an accident and I had to stop.
  • What did you used to do?

7/26 It's just happened

Just

● Immediate past
● Very short time ago

Just sirve para referirse a una acción reciente, pasado inmediato o que ocurrió hace un corto periodo de tiempo y se utiliza en conjunto con la estructura gramatical present perfect.

Estructura: subject + have/has + just + past participle.

Ejemplos usando just

  • I’m good, thanks. I have just eaten. (acabo de comer)
  • Joanna has just finished the report. (acaba de terminar)
  • The kids have just woken up. (acaban de despertar)
  • We could make it! He has just started his presentation. (acaba de empezar)

Ejercicios de writing

9/26 By preposition

By

● Express agency
● by + agent

By es una preposición que se utiliza para definir a la persona o a la cosa que realiza la acción.

Ejemplos usando by

  • Last month I read a great book by Virginia Woolf.
  • The building was restored by the constructor.
  • It’s a stunning painting by Picasso.

10/26 To preposition

● Refer to the recipient.
● Refer to the indirect object.
● to + noun.

To es una preposición que se utiliza para varios casos, uno de estos es definir a la persona o a la cosa que recibe algo.

Ejemplos usando to

  • We’ll give this to your mother, not to you. Don’t insist, please.
  • Sally explained everything to her siblings.

12/26 Possessive Forms


● Something belongs to someone.
● Used without a noun.
● Vary according to the owner.
● Avoid repeating words.

Possessive forms son formas gramaticales para referirse a la pertenencia o posesión de algo. Se utilizan tres formas principales:

  • Los pronombres posesivos (possessive pronouns)
  • El apóstrofe 's
  • La preposición of

Possessive pronouns

Los pronombres posesivos sustituyen al sustantivo que le pertenece un objeto. Los puntos más importantes para utilizar los possessive pronouns son:

  • Hace referencia a algo que pertenece a alguien
  • Sustituye al sustantivo
  • Varía de acuerdo con el propietario
  • Evita la repetición de palabras
Subject Adjective Possessive
I my mine
You your yours
He his his
She her hers
It its its*
We our ours
You your yours
They their theirs

*its es muy poco común

Ejemplos usando possessive pronouns

❌ Can you believe I bought a car? It’s my car!
✅ Can you believe I bought a car? It’s mine!

❌ This is my pen. Your pen is over there.
✅ This is my pen. Yours is over there.

❌ His dog is white. Her dog is brown.
✅ His dog is white. Hers is brown.

Apóstrofe 's

El apóstrofe ’s se añade al sustantivo que le pertenece a un objeto. Si el sustantivo termina en (-s) entonces solo se añade el apóstrofe al sustantivo.

Estructura: subject + 's + object.

Ejemplos usando el apóstrofe 's

  • Jane is one of Mary’s cousins. (primos de Mary)
  • This park is part of the city’s project. (proyecto de la ciudad)
  • Their living room is the size of John’s entire apartment. (departamento de John)

Preposición of

La preposición of sirve para referirse al sustantivo perteneciente al objeto utilizando un possessive pronoun o un apóstrofe 's.

Ejemplos usando of

✅ Jane is one of Mary’s cousins.
✅ Jane is a cousin of Mary’s.
❌ Jane is a cousin of Mary.
✅ Jane is a cousin of hers.
❌ Jane is a cousin of her.

✅ She is one of my co-workers.
✅ She is a co-worker of mine.

Ejercicio de writing

13/26 Compound pronouns: anyone, anybody, anything y anywhere

Los pronombres impersonales compuestos son aquellos que se refieren a personas, lugares o cosas de manera general.
Aunque hacen referencia a un grupo, se les considera como tercera persona en singular (como he, she, it ), esto es importante para la conjugación del verbo.

Puedes revisar la clase del curso de inglés básico A2: Verbo to be, Adjetivos y Superlativos, para un vistazo general de los pronombres impersonales compuestos.

Hoy nos enfocaremos en los pronombres impersonales formados por el prefijo any-.

Anyone y anybody

Anyone y anybody son pronombres impersonales compuestos que se utilizan para un grupo general de personas, no importa quién.

  • Don’t worry. I won’t tell anybody!
  • He can invite anyone to his graduation party.
  • Sam didn’t find anyone to go to the movies.
  • Was there anybody at the park?

Anything

Anything es un pronombre impersonal compuesto que se utiliza para un grupo general de cosas, no importa qué.

  • You know you can say anything to me.
  • We would give anything to be there now.
  • I’m bored! I don’t have anything to do at home.
  • Is there anything to eat in this house?

Anywhere

Anywhere es un pronombre impersonal compuesto que se utiliza para un lugar en general, no importa dónde.

  • Sally could go anywhere, but she came here!
  • We didn’t go anywhere last summer.
  • Do you need to be anywhere else?

15/26 Like... better / more than...

Like… better / more than… es una estructura para describir preferencias, es decir, que te gusta más una cosa, persona o lugar, sobre otra.

Ejemplos usando like … more/better than

  • I like cats better than dogs.
  • Terry likes running more than swimming.
  • My kids like pasta better than rice.
  • Do you like soccer more than basketball?

16/26 (Not) Same ... as ...

Same as (igual que) es una estructura para describir comparaciones o similitudes. Se puede utilizar un adjetivo entre ambas palabras seguida de la comparación.

Ejemplos usando same as

Oraciones positivas:

  • The time in Miami is the same as in Bogotá.
  • The word “rain” is pronounced the same way as “reign”.
  • Your house is the same size as mine.

Oraciones negativas:

  • The time in Miami isn’t the same as in Bogotá.
  • The word “rain” is not pronounced the same way as “reign”.
  • Your house is not the same size as mine.

17/26 Comparison and preferences

Lee el texto y presta atención al uso de (not) (the) same as y like better/more than:

I have lived in two countries in South America: Brazil and Colombia. Every country has its particularities, but at the same time, they have more things in common than we imagine.

Living in Brazil is not the same as living in Colombia, but you can get used to it fast. People in Colombia are friendly. They can make you feel good in every environment. Also, there is great food there, but I still like Brazilian food better than Colombian. The flavors are not quite the same.

The economy is also similar in both countries, but Brazil is more expensive. You can earn the same working in both countries, but you end up spending more in Brazil. So, in this way, I like living in Colombia more than in Brazil.

Another aspect I can mention is the weather. Brazil is such a big country that you can find all kinds of weather. Some cities are hotter than others. Some others are really cold during winter. Colombia is not the same as Brazil in this aspect. You don’t feel the weather change very much there. If the city is hot, it is like that the entire year. The same happens if the city is cold.

It’s hard to choose one I like better than the other.

18/26 Changing situations: present continuous and verbs of state change

Para hablar sobre situaciones o estados cambiantes se utiliza la estructura gramatical present continuous y los verbos de cambio de estado (verbs of state change).

Ejemplos usando present continuous

Con la estructura gramatical present continuous, se puede expresar algo que está cambiando:

  • Sally is getting taller.
  • Food prices are going up.
  • It is getting colder in here.
  • We should go. I am getting hungry.
  • Winters are becoming warmer.

Ejemplos usando verbos de estado cambiante

Verbs of state change son verbos que naturalmente indican que algo cambia:

  • We painted the wall white.
  • I want to make this clear.
  • The robbers broke the lock open.
  • Sandra made everything better.

Ejercicio de writing

19/26 Suggestions and offers with "shall"

Shall I/we …? y What / How about …?* son dos estructuras para realizar sugerencias, ofrecimientos o invitaciones. Para responder a estas preguntas, podemos utilizar Yes, let’s.

Shall I / we …?

Los puntos más importantes para utilizar Shall I / we …? son:

  • La estructura contiene el verbo infinitivo.
  • Shall I …? se usa para ofrecer algo.
  • Shall we …? se usa para realizar sugerencias o invitaciones.
  • No es común utilizar con los demás pronombres personales (you, he, etc.).

Estructura: Shall I / we + verb in infitive + complement ?

Ejemplos usando Shall I / we … ?

  • Shall I help you with the essay?
  • Shall we go to the supermarket?
  • Shall I buy a new one?
  • Shall we stay home?
  • Shall I prepare dinner?

What / How about …?

Los puntos más importantes para utilizar What / How about …? son:

  • La estructura contiene el verbo en present participle (-ing).
  • Se utiliza para realizar sugerencias.

Estructura: What / How about + verb in present participle (-ing) + complement ?

Ejemplos usando What / How about …?

  • What about helping them?
  • How about going to the supermarket?
  • What about buying a new one?
  • How about staying home?
  • What about preparing dinner?

Yes, let’s

Yes, let’s se utiliza para responder preguntas sobre sugerencias y ofrecimientos.

(?) Shall we go to the supermarket?
(+) Yes, let’s.

(?) What about preparing dinner?
(+) Yes, let’s.

  • He invited us to a party.

21/26 A bit / A little / A few

A bit, a little y a few son cuantificadores que sirven para referirse a cantidades pequeñas. A few se utiliza para sustantivos contables. A bit y a little se utiliza para sustantivos incontables. Los tres cuantificadores son sinónimos de some.

Ejemplos usando a few

  • I had a few ideas for the project.
  • She brought a few apples, so I can make a pie.
  • They spent a few days in New York last year.

Ejemplos usando a little o a bit

  • There is a little milk left. You don’t need to buy more.
  • Of course, you can have a bit of cake.
  • He has a little time to rest.

Ejercicio de writing

Escribe cinco oraciones usando los cuantificadores a few, a little o a bit.

22/26 Too / Enough

Too y enough son palabras que se usan para mejorar o limitar el significado de un adverbio.

Los adverbios de modo (adverbs of manner) indican cómo se realiza una acción y se dividen en regulares, que se forman añadiendo -ly al adjetivo; y en irregulares, que varían con respecto al adjetivo.

Regular Irregular
Quickly High
Carefully Late
Softly Hard
Loudly Well
Badly Early
Exactly Wrong
Easily Fast
Politely Straight
Equally Daily

Too

Too hace referencia a que algo es más que suficiente (demasiado rápido) o menos que la necesaria (demasiado lento), siempre en una oración positiva en un contexto negativo.

Estructura: Too + adverb of manner.

Ejemplos usando too

  • He speaks too slowly. I’m bored!
  • This kid is eating too quickly.
  • You are listening to this music too loudly.
  • Be careful! You drive too fast.

Enough

Enough se utiliza para referirse a la cantidad de algo, es suficiente en una oración positiva, o insuficiente en una oración negativa.

Estructura: Adverb of manner + enough.

  • Erick doesn’t work hard enough.
  • I’m feeling well enough to go.
  • He’s driving fast enough to win the race.
  • They are not walking quickly enough.

Ejercicio de writing

Escribe cuatro oraciones usando to y enough para adverbios de modo.

23/26 Definite / Indefinite / No Article

El artículo es una palabra que acompaña al sustantivo para expresar si es conocido (artículo definido: the) o desconocido (artículo indefinido: a / an).

Artículo definido the

El artículo definido the expresa una generalidad para un sustantivo singular o plural. ¿Cuándo usarlo? En los siguientes casos:

  • Un sustantivo particular que ya ha sido mencionado.
  • Algo único, solo existe uno (ej: the President).
  • Persona particular (ej: the manager)
  • Grupos de personas (ej: the spanish).
  • Familias (ej: The Smith’s)
  • Números ordinales y decadas (ej: the first, the second)
  • Instrumentos musicales. (ej: the piano)
  • Lugar particular (ej: the house).
  • Famosos monumentos, edificios, museos, etc. (ej: The Eiffel Tower).
  • Hoteles, bares y restaurantes, sin ser nombrados después de una persona (ej: the hotel ✅, the Hilton Hotel ❌)
  • Áreas geográficas, grupos de ríos, cordilleras, océanos (ej: The Andes).
  • Ciudades en plural, repúblicas, reinos, estados (ej: The United States, The Philippines).

Artículos indefinidos a y an

El artículo definido the expresa unidad o singularidad para un sustantivo. Si un sustantivo termina con vocal se utilizará an en lugar de a. ¿Cuándo utilizarlos? En siguientes casos:

  • Referirse a algo por primera vez.
  • Miembro inespecífico de un grupo, no es una persona particular (ej: a doctor).
  • Indicar un solo elemento (ej: an apple)

Ten cuidado de utilizar a o an para sustantivos en plural:
❌ an apples.
✅ some apples.

  • Clase de artículos indefinidos del curso de inglés avanzado.

Oraciones sin artículo

Las oraciones sin artículo son aquellas que el sustantivo no necesita especificar si es conocido o desconocido. ¿Cuándo usarlo? En los siguientes casos:

  • Ideas generales, que significan “todo” o “en general” (ej: Life is beautiful).
  • Nombres propios o títulos seguidos por nombres (ej, Andres, Dr. Andres).
  • Libros y canciones (ej: “Don Quijote de la Mancha”)
  • Ciudades y países, que no estén en plural (ej: Mexico).
  • Planetas (ej: Venus).
  • Calles, parques, estados, continentes (ej: America).
  • Áreas de estudio (ej: Biology).
  • Idiomas (ej: Spanish).
  • Comidas (ej: breakfast).
  • Deportes y juegos (ej: soccer).
  • Lagos, islas, montañas en singular (ej: Everest).
  • Sustantivos abstractos (ej: money).

Ejercicio de writing

Escribe dos oraciones para cada forma del artículo (definido, indefinido y sin artículo).

Ejercicio alternativo de reading

Grocery shopping

Person 1: Rachel and the kids are coming for lunch today. We have to prepare something special.
Person 2: Oh! I had forgotten about that. You’re right. What about cooking some seafood? It’s delicious.
Person 1: Yeah! It’s delicious. But it’s not easy to prepare. How about making pasta? It’s easier to cook, and I like it more than seafood.
Person 2: Ok, then. Pasta it is. Shall we go grocery shopping?
Person 1: First, we need a shopping list… We need to buy a few things.
Person 2: Right! We need to buy spaghetti, an onion, some garlic, a little salt, and pepper… (speaking fast)
Person 1: Slow down! You are speaking too fast. (pretending to write)
Person 2: Sorry!
Person 1: (pretending to finish the list) salt and pepper… The tomato sauce! We should buy the canned one!
Person 2: Of course not! Preparing a fresh tomato sauce is not the same as buying one. Fresh tomatoes make a more delicious sauce.
Person 1: Ok! We can buy fresh tomatoes if you insist. But YOU cook the sauce! I don’t cook well enough…
Person 2: No problem. Do you have a big pan? We need a big one. Three people are coming, plus us two…
Person 1: Sure! Like the big one over there?
Person 2: Nice! I’m getting hungry already (hahaha).
Person 1: Me too! I believe we have everything we need on the list. And if not, we can add a few items to the list when we get to the grocery store. Shall we go now?
Person 2: Yes, let’s.
